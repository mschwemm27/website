Collaborative Learning at the Interface of Mathematics and Biology

  • Year long program where I assisted in the mentoring of seven scholarship undergraduate students with backgrounds in mathematics and biology. The students learned the concepts behind mathematical biology through a series of research modules which illustrated the application of mathematical techniques in a variety of biological fields.

  • Aided the students in the formulation and pursuit of their own research project during the summer which involved modeling the effects of age structure and voluntary vaccination on outbreaks of measles epidemics in the United Kingdom. Here are the slides from the students' end of the year presentation on their work.

  • Being involved in this program allowed me to not only gain experience teaching mathematics to students with very diverse backgrounds, but also exposed me to a wide variety of topics in mathematical biology. I highly recommend this program to any undergraduates interested in mathematical biology as well as graduate students with a mathematical background interested in mentoring motivated students with varied backgrounds in mathematics.

  • One of the research modules the students were involved in prior to the development of their own research project grew into a manuscript which was recently submitted for publication (Constraints on the use of lifespan shortening Wolbachia to control dengue fever )
